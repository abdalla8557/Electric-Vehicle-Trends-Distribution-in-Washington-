

# ⚡ Electric Vehicle Trends & Distribution in Washington
### *Analyzing the Rise of Electric Vehicles and Market Dynamics*

---

##  Project Overview

This Power BI dashboard project provides a comprehensive analysis of **Electric Vehicle (EV) adoption and distribution across Washington State**. It explores market trends, geographic spread, vehicle type breakdowns, and brand dominance — offering actionable insights into how EVs are reshaping the automotive landscape.

---

##  Dashboard Pages

### 1.  Electric Vehicles Overview
A high-level summary of the entire EV ecosystem in Washington:
- **251K** Total Electric Vehicles registered
- **200K** Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs)
- **51K** Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs)
- **44.07 mi** Average Electric Range
- Top 5 EV makes by maximum electric range: **Tesla (337 mi)**, Porsche (308 mi), Chevrolet (259 mi), Hyundai (258 mi), Kia (239 mi)
- BEVs make up **79.61%** of all EVs vs. 20.39% PHEVs
- Growth trend of EVs from **2000 to 2026**, with a sharp surge peaking around **2023–2024**

---

### 2.  Geographic Insights (Distribution by Location)
Spatial breakdown of EV ownership across Washington cities:
- **Seattle** leads with ~**40K** EVs, followed by Bellevue (12K), Vancouver (9K), and Redmond (9K)
- Interactive **Bing Map** showing vehicle density across Washington State
- Top car brands distributed across cities: **Tesla dominates** (~630 cities), followed by Chevrolet, Ford, Kia, and Nissan

---

### 3.  Vehicle Market Breakdown
Deep dive into the EV market composition:
- **Model Y** is the top-selling EV model with **52K** units, followed by Model 3 (37K) and Nissan Leaf (14K)
- **Tesla** dominates the brand market with **105K** vehicles — nearly **6x** the next competitor (Chevrolet at 18K)
- Clean Fuel Vehicle Eligibility breakdown:
  - ✅ **60.7%** — Clean Alternative Fuel Vehicle Eligible (152.16K)
  - ⚠️ **29.92%** — Eligibility unknown (74.99K)
  - ❌ **9.38%** — Not eligible due to low battery range (23.52K)

## 🔍 Key Insights

1. **Tesla's Market Dominance** — Tesla holds an overwhelming share of the EV market, with its Model Y and Model 3 together accounting for over 35% of all EVs in Washington.
2. **BEVs Over PHEVs** — Nearly 80% of EVs in Washington are fully battery-powered, signaling a strong shift toward pure electric mobility.
3. **Urban Concentration** — EV adoption is heavily concentrated in the Seattle metro area, with Seattle alone having over 3x the EVs of the second-ranked city.
4. **Explosive Growth Post-2020** — EV registrations saw exponential growth from 2020 onward, reflecting national incentives, infrastructure improvements, and growing consumer interest.
5. **Clean Fuel Eligibility Gap** — About 30% of registered EVs have unknown clean fuel eligibility status, suggesting a need for better data collection and classification.


---

## 📌 Data Source

This analysis is based on the publicly available **Electric Vehicle Population Dataset** from Washington State — tracking all registered EVs with attributes including make, model, type, range, and city of registration.
